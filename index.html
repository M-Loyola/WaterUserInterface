Importance of Water

Keeping hydrated is crucial for health and well-being, but many people do not consume enough fluids each day.
Around 60 percent of the body is made up of water, and around 71 percent of the planet's surface is covered by water. Perhaps it is the ubiquitous nature of water that means drinking enough each day is not at the top of many people's lists of priorities.
  1. Delivers oxygen throughout the body

    2. Blood is more than 90 percent water, and blood carries oxygen to different parts of the body.

  2. Cushions the brain, spinal cord, and other sensitive tissues

    3. Dehydration can affect brain structure and function. It is also involved in the production of hormones and neurotransmitters. Prolonged dehydration can lead to problems with thinking and reasoning.

  3. Regulates body temperature

    4. Water that is stored in the middle layers of the skin comes to the skin's surface as sweat when the body heats up. As it evaporates, it cools the body. In sport.

  4. The digestive system depends on it

    5. The bowel needs water to work properly. Dehydration can lead to digestive problems, constipation, and an overly acidic stomach. This increases the risk of heartburn and stomach ulcers.

  5. Flushes body waste

    6. Water is needed in the processes of sweating and removal of urine and feces.

  6. It makes minerals and nutrients accessible

    7. These dissolve in water, which makes it possible for them to reach different parts of the body.
